# Cyberly Content Taxonomy And Roadmap

## Purpose

Cyberly needs structured content so its learning experience can scale safely and consistently. The content taxonomy gives Cyberly a shared way to organise Resource learning, Scenario practice, RAG grounding, deterministic action cards, and Agentic AI learning-route planning.

Without a taxonomy, CyberGuard can still answer questions, but it becomes harder to choose the right Resource, match a Scenario to a learner's topic, explain recommendations, or identify content gaps. With a taxonomy, each content item can be connected to a topic, learner level, relationship type, source, review status, and future Admin workflow.

This document is based on the workbook planning sheets:

- `02_Content_Inventory`
- `03_Content_Relationships`
- `Sheet3`, which contains the `Cyberly Content Category Plan` corresponding to the requested `08_Content_Category_Plan`

## Current Content Foundation

The current content inventory is organised around four main content types:

- Resources
- Scenarios
- FAQs
- Safety Summaries

The workbook shows a balanced MVP planning base across seven existing topic groups. Each topic currently has planned coverage for Resources, one Scenario, FAQs, and one Safety Summary. The inventory is designed to support CyberGuard responses, RAG retrieval, action cards, and future learning-route planning.

The current MVP content foundation includes topics for:

- Scams
- Misinformation
- AI and technology safety
- Privacy
- Online safety
- Password safety
- Beginner digital foundations

Resources and Safety Summaries are especially useful for RAG because they provide reviewed explanatory content. FAQs are useful for common learner questions. Scenarios support practice and action cards after learners read or review a topic.

## Recommended Main Content Categories

The workbook category plan recommends seven main content categories:

- Beginner / Digital Foundations
- Scams & Social Engineering
- Passwords & Account Security
- Privacy & Personal Data Protection
- Online Safety & Digital Wellbeing
- Misinformation & Media Literacy
- AI & Technology Safety

These categories should become the main planning structure for future Cyberly content expansion.

## Category Details

### Beginner / Digital Foundations

Purpose: provide the entry-level cyber wellness foundation before learners move into more specific risks.

Target learner problem: some learners may not yet understand basic digital citizenship, safe online habits, or how manipulation works online.

Example subcategories:

- Digital Citizenship
- Social Engineering Basics
- Safe Digital Habits
- Think Before Clicking
- Asking Trusted Adults / Teachers
- Respectful Online Behaviour

Cyber Wellness support: this category helps learners build safe everyday habits, respectful behaviour, and confidence to ask for help.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: beginner content can be used as a prerequisite or remedial route step for low-confidence learners. It can help CyberGuard explain concepts simply before suggesting harder Scams, Password, Privacy, or Safety activities.

### Scams & Social Engineering

Purpose: teach learners how scams work, how manipulation happens, and how to respond safely.

Target learner problem: learners may receive phishing links, fake QR codes, scam calls, fake shopping offers, or urgent requests for OTPs and money.

Example subcategories:

- Phishing
- Online Financial Scams
- QR Code Scams
- SIM Swap / OTP Fraud
- Catfishing
- Crypto / Investment Scams
- Ransomware Awareness

Cyber Wellness support: this category supports practical scam recognition, safer decision-making, and Malaysia-relevant response guidance.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: this is a strong demo area for CyberGuard because phishing has Resource, Scenario, FAQ, and Safety Summary coverage. Agentic routes can move learners from a scam Resource into a phishing/social-engineering Scenario and then Progress review.

### Passwords & Account Security

Purpose: teach learners how to protect accounts and respond to account-security warning signs.

Target learner problem: learners may reuse passwords, share OTPs, ignore suspicious login alerts, or feel unsure what to do after account compromise.

Example subcategories:

- Strong Passwords
- Multi-Factor Authentication
- OTP Protection
- Password Managers
- Account Recovery Safety
- Suspicious Login Alerts

Cyber Wellness support: this category builds core defensive habits for email, social media, gaming, school, and financial accounts.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: password content supports safe defensive prompts such as "How do I protect my password?" and can be used for remedial learning routes after weak account-security evidence.

### Privacy & Personal Data Protection

Purpose: help learners understand personal data, privacy risks, and safer sharing behaviour.

Target learner problem: learners may overshare personal information, use risky app permissions, connect to public Wi-Fi carelessly, or expose location and identity details.

Example subcategories:

- Personal Data
- Public Wi-Fi Safety
- Digital Footprint
- Identity Theft
- App Permissions
- Social Media Privacy
- E-Wallet / Online Banking Safety
- Location Sharing

Cyber Wellness support: this category connects daily online behaviour with long-term safety, reputation, and data-protection awareness.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: privacy content can ground RAG answers, support internal Resource actions, and create routes such as Resource -> Scenario -> Progress for public Wi-Fi, social media privacy, or app permissions.

### Online Safety & Digital Wellbeing

Purpose: address online harm, support-seeking, respectful behaviour, and teen digital wellbeing.

Target learner problem: learners may face cyberbullying, grooming, sextortion, doxxing, unsafe stranger interactions, gaming risks, or emotional harm online.

Example subcategories:

- Cyberbullying
- Online Grooming
- Sextortion
- Doxxing
- Online Gaming Safety
- Stranger Interaction
- Digital Wellbeing
- Reporting Harm

Cyber Wellness support: this category expands Cyberly beyond technical security into safety, wellbeing, and trusted-adult support.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: content in this category should be carefully reviewed and support-oriented. CyberGuard can use it for safe escalation guidance, but high-risk topics should avoid graphic detail and should recommend trusted adult, teacher, guardian, platform, or official support where appropriate.

### Misinformation & Media Literacy

Purpose: teach learners how to evaluate online information before believing or sharing it.

Target learner problem: learners may share fake news, clickbait, emotionally manipulative posts, or unverified images and videos.

Example subcategories:

- Fake News
- Fact-Checking Tools
- Responsible Sharing
- Clickbait
- Emotional Manipulation
- Source Evaluation
- Image / Video Verification

Cyber Wellness support: this category builds critical thinking, responsible sharing, and safer participation in online communities.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: misinformation content is useful for RAG answers, fact-checking learning routes, and practice Scenarios where learners decide whether to share viral content.

### AI & Technology Safety

Purpose: help learners understand AI-related online risks and use AI tools safely.

Target learner problem: learners may encounter deepfakes, AI-generated scams, voice cloning, synthetic media, or privacy risks when using AI chatbots.

Example subcategories:

- Deepfakes
- AI-Generated Content
- AI-Enabled Phishing
- Voice Cloning Scams
- AI Chatbot Privacy
- Synthetic Media Verification
- Prompt / Data Leakage Basics

Cyber Wellness support: this category prepares learners for emerging technology risks while keeping the guidance defensive and age-appropriate.

CyberGuard / RAG / Agentic AI support: AI safety content is especially useful because Cyberly itself uses AI. CyberGuard can teach learners not to paste private data into AI tools, how to question synthetic media, and how to verify AI-generated scam messages.

## Priority Content Expansion Plan

### Phase 1: Stabilize Current MVP Content

Goals:

- Verify source URLs.
- Verify source organisations.
- Confirm reviewed, approved, and `rag_ready` status.
- Ensure each topic has Resource, Scenario, FAQ, and Safety Summary support.
- Check that CyberGuard citations use credible reviewed sources.
- Confirm that Malaysia-specific claims are backed by official or suitable reviewed sources.

This phase protects the current demo and makes the existing RAG foundation more reliable.

### Phase 2: Expand High-Priority Subcategories

Recommended expansion topics:

- QR code scams
- SIM swap / OTP fraud
- Online shopping scams
- Password manager basics
- Suspicious login alerts
- App permissions
- Social media privacy
- Doxxing
- Online gaming safety
- AI-generated scam messages
- AI chatbot privacy

Each topic should ideally receive:

- At least one Resource.
- A short FAQ set for common learner questions.
- A Safety Summary where the topic has safety or escalation implications.
- A Scenario when the topic involves a practical decision or judgement moment.

### Phase 3: Improve Learning-Route Relationships

The taxonomy should support explicit relationship types:

- `prerequisite`
- `next_step`
- `practice_after`
- `remedial`
- `related_topic`

These relationships can help CyberGuard build clearer learning routes. For example:

- Beginner "Think Before Clicking" can prepare learners for phishing.
- A phishing Resource can lead to a social-engineering Scenario.
- A failed or low-confidence Scenario can lead to a Safety Summary.
- A password Resource can lead to OTP protection or suspicious login alerts.

### Phase 4: Prepare For Admin Content Workflow

Future Admin workflow should support:

- Content review.
- Source metadata.
- `rag_ready` toggle.
- Reviewer and review date.
- Next review date.
- Malaysia guidance validation.
- Content gap tracking.
- Relationship management.

This would turn the taxonomy from a planning document into a maintainable content governance system.

## Relationship To `03_Content_Relationships`

The `03_Content_Relationships` sheet defines how content items can connect to each other. The relationship examples include:

- `prepare_before`
- `next_step`
- `practice_after`
- `remedial`

These relationships support:

- Action cards: CyberGuard can recommend the next safe internal action after an answer.
- Learning route planning: Agentic AI can build Resource -> Scenario -> Progress style routes.
- Remedial recommendations: weaker or incorrect Scenario performance can lead to Safety Summaries or beginner content.
- RAG topic linking: FAQs can answer a question, then point toward a deeper Resource.
- Scenario-after-resource practice: a Resource can prepare learners for a Scenario in the same topic.

The relationship sheet already models common patterns such as Safety Summary -> Resource, Resource -> Resource, Resource -> Scenario, Scenario -> Safety Summary, and FAQ -> Resource/Safety Summary.

## Relationship To CyberGuard RAG

CyberGuard RAG should use reviewed knowledge sources carefully.

Best RAG sources:

- Resources, because they contain explanatory learning content.
- Safety Summaries, because they provide concise reviewed guidance.
- FAQs, because they answer common learner questions directly.

Scenarios are useful for practice and action cards, but raw scenario attempts, selected decisions, and learner-specific Scenario histories should not be used as RAG knowledge.

Malaysia-specific guidance should require official reviewed sources. CyberGuard should not invent hotline details, legal claims, official procedures, or emergency contacts. If Malaysia response guidance is added later, it should include source organisation, source URL, reviewed date, and next review date.

## Relationship To Agentic AI

Agentic AI route planning needs structured categories and relationships.

The current route pattern can combine:

- Resource
- Scenario
- Progress

Future route planning can become stronger by using:

- `prerequisite` relationships for beginner routes.
- `practice_after` relationships for Scenario recommendations.
- `remedial` relationships after weak performance.
- `next_step` relationships for progressive learning.
- `related_topic` relationships for cross-topic guidance.

The system should avoid completed Scenarios when alternatives exist and should avoid recommending unreviewed, draft, archived, or non-RAG-ready content.

## Future Admin Use

This taxonomy can become the basis for Admin content management.

Possible Admin features:

- Admin Resource Management.
- Admin Scenario Management.
- FAQ and Safety Summary management.
- RAG-ready approval workflow.
- Source review and source metadata management.
- Content gap tracking.
- Category and subcategory assignment.
- Content relationship editing.
- Malaysia guidance review.
- Reviewer, reviewed date, and next review date tracking.

The taxonomy gives Admin users a structured way to see what Cyberly has, what is missing, and what should be reviewed before CyberGuard uses it.

## Report-Ready Summary

Cyberly's content taxonomy is important because the platform is no longer only a set of static pages. It now supports AI chat, RAG grounding, deterministic action cards, Scenario practice, and Agentic AI learning-route planning. These features require content to be organised by topic, category, learner level, review status, and relationship to other content.

The proposed taxonomy groups Cyberly content into seven learner-friendly categories: Beginner / Digital Foundations, Scams & Social Engineering, Passwords & Account Security, Privacy & Personal Data Protection, Online Safety & Digital Wellbeing, Misinformation & Media Literacy, and AI & Technology Safety. This structure supports cyber wellness by covering both technical security habits and social/emotional online safety risks faced by teenagers.

The taxonomy also supports future system growth. It can guide Admin content management, help identify missing topics, improve RAG source quality, and allow CyberGuard to build safer learning routes such as Resource -> Scenario -> Progress. By treating content as reviewed, structured learning material rather than isolated pages, Cyberly can provide more reliable AI-assisted cybersecurity education.

## Current Limitations

- Workbook planning is not yet connected to an Admin UI.
- Some subcategories are missing content.
- Resource completion tracking is not implemented.
- Malaysia guidance requires official reviewed sources.
- Content relationships need future validation by educators or reviewers.
- Some workbook topic labels may need normalization before direct database use.
- Exact production coverage may differ from workbook planning if database content changes.
